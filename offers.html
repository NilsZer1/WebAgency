STEP 01

Initial consultation:
The first step is typically an initial consultation between
the client and the supplier. This can happen over the phone,
email, or video conference. During this consultation, the
supplier should ask the client questions about their business,
goals, target audience, and preferences for social media and
web design. The supplier should also explain their services
and provide examples of their work to give the client an idea
of what they can expect.

STEP 02

Proposal:
After the initial consultation, the supplier should prepare a
proposal that outlines the scope of work, timeline, and pricing
for the project. This proposal should be detailed enough to give
the client a clear understanding of what they will be getting,
but not so detailed that it overwhelms them. The proposal can be
delivered via email or video conference, and the supplier should
be available to answer any questions the client may have.

STEP 03

Agreement:
Once the proposal has been accepted, the supplier should prepare
a written agreement that outlines the terms and conditions of the
project. This agreement should be signed by both parties before any
work begins. The supplier can deliver the agreement via email or
video conference and should be available to explain any terms that
the client may not understand.

STEP 04

Design and development:
With the agreement in place, the supplier can begin the design
and development phase of the project. During this phase, the
supplier should keep the client updated on progress and provide
opportunities for feedback and revisions. This can be done via
email, video conference, or a project management tool.

STEP 05

Payment and delivery:
Once the project is complete, the supplier should deliver the
final product to the client and request payment. This can be
done via email or video conference, and the supplier should be
available to answer any questions or address any concerns the
client may have. If the client is satisfied with the final
product, they can make the payment, and the project will be
considered complete.
